Why It Matters

The Stakes Are Real

Pennsylvania's water challenges are not abstract. They shape public health, community resilience, native wildlife, and the long-term livability of the region. Understanding the problem is where meaningful action begins.

⚠

Legacy pollution continues to impair Pennsylvania streams

Abandoned mine drainage from centuries of coal extraction still acidifies hundreds of miles of Pennsylvania waterways — largely unaddressed and underreported.

◔

Clean water supports communities, recreation, and ecosystems

Healthy watersheds underpin drinking water systems, outdoor economies, agricultural irrigation, and the ecological fabric that holds Pennsylvania communities together.

◌

Brook trout depend on cold, healthy headwaters

Pennsylvania's native brook trout are a sentinel species — their presence signals water quality and thermal integrity. Their disappearance is a warning we cannot afford to ignore.

❉

Restoration strengthens environmental resilience

Science-based stream restoration rebuilds biological communities, stabilizes stream banks, reduces flood vulnerability, and restores the natural capacity of watersheds to self-heal.

About the Initiative

Studying the Problems Others Overlook

Keystone Water Initiative exists to study overlooked environmental problems that affect communities, ecosystems, and public health across Pennsylvania and the broader Appalachian region. Acid mine drainage from thousands of abandoned coal and iron mines continues to silently poison streams, destroy aquatic habitat, and contaminate groundwater — yet receives a fraction of the attention it deserves.

We apply civil and environmental engineering methodology, rigorous water quality analysis, and GIS-based spatial tools to document the scale of this crisis and develop practical paths toward restoration. Our work is independent, data-driven, and publicly available.

Current Focus Areas

Where the Work Happens

Our current efforts concentrate on four interconnected areas of environmental research, monitoring, engineering, and public communication — all aimed at understanding and addressing Pennsylvania's most pressing water quality challenges.

💧
Active Research

Acid Mine Drainage Research

Field investigation and chemical analysis of AMD-impacted stream reaches across Pennsylvania's anthracite and bituminous coal regions. Documenting metal loading, pH depression, and biological impairment.

  • pH & Conductivity Monitoring
  • Dissolved Metals Analysis
  • Biological Assessments
  • Discharge Characterization
🗺
Ongoing

Watershed Monitoring

Systematic documentation of water quality conditions, land use impacts, and ecological indicators across priority Pennsylvania watershed systems — building a baseline dataset for long-term analysis.

  • Stream Macroinvertebrates
  • Water Temperature Logging
  • GIS Data Collection
  • Impairment Mapping
⚙
Planning Phase

Restoration Strategy Evaluation

Assessment of passive and active treatment approaches for AMD-impaired streams, including constructed wetlands, anoxic limestone drains, and land reclamation feasibility studies.

  • Passive Treatment Concepts
  • Wetland Construction Design
  • Cost-Benefit Analysis
  • Site Prioritization
⌂
Active

Public Education & Awareness

Translating technical research into accessible public content — communicating the causes and consequences of watershed degradation to communities, students, advocates, and decision-makers.

  • Science Communication
  • Community Outreach
  • Environmental Reporting
  • Policy Briefings
